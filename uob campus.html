Chapter 02

The Problem:
Invisible Drop-Off

The team knew students weren't fully activating, but lacked the behavioural resolution to know where, why, or how severe. Feature adoption data was surfaced by gut feel, not by evidence.

⚠️

The Core Gap

Without cohort-level SQL analysis, the team couldn't distinguish between students who hadn't discovered a feature and students who'd tried it and abandoned it — two completely different problems requiring opposite solutions.

📊

What Was Missing

Granular session-level behavioural data segmented by onboarding cohort, feature area, and user journey stage. Without this, roadmap decisions defaulted to stakeholder opinion.

Cohort onboarding funnel — drop-off by feature area SQL · PostgreSQL 
-- Identify session drop-off by onboarding stage and feature area
-- Cohort: students who installed within first 2 weeks of term

WITH onboarding_cohort AS (
  SELECT
    user_id,
    DATE_TRUNC('week', first_session_at) AS cohort_week,
    MAX(CASE WHEN feature = 'timetable' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS used_timetable,
    MAX(CASE WHEN feature = 'student_services' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS used_services,
    MAX(CASE WHEN feature = 'campus_map' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS used_map,
    COUNT(DISTINCT session_id) AS total_sessions
  FROM app_events
  WHERE first_session_at BETWEEN '2023-09-01' AND '2023-09-14'
  GROUP BY 1, 2
),
retention_30d AS (
  SELECT
    c.user_id,
    CASE WHEN MAX(e.event_at) >= c.first_session_at + INTERVAL '30 days'
         THEN 1 ELSE 0 END AS retained_30d
  FROM onboarding_cohort c
  JOIN app_events e USING (user_id)
  GROUP BY 1, c.first_session_at
)
SELECT
  oc.cohort_week,
  COUNT(*) AS cohort_size,
  AVG(r.retained_30d) AS retention_rate,
  SUM(oc.used_services) * 1.0 / COUNT(*) AS services_activation_rate,
  AVG(oc.total_sessions) AS avg_sessions
FROM onboarding_cohort oc
JOIN retention_30d r USING (user_id)
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY 1;
💡

What the queries revealed

Students who activated the student services module within their first 3 sessions had 2.4× higher 30-day retention than those who didn't — but only 19% of the cohort found and used it within that window. The issue was discoverability, not product-market fit.

The Key Insight

The biggest single leverage point wasn't a broken feature — it was a discoverability problem in the onboarding architecture. 81% of students who never found student services went on to churn within 30 days.

📈

Why This Mattered

Students who used student services in their first 3 sessions had 2.4× higher 30-day retention. Moving activation from 19% → 40% would retain roughly 7,500 additional students per intake cohort.

Chapter 05

Recommendations & Roadmap Input

Findings were translated into structured product recommendations — not vague data observations — and delivered to the product lead for direct inclusion in the quarterly roadmap review.

1

Resolve timetable first-load failure via async ID pre-fetch

The timetable's ID sync delay caused 22% of new students to see a blank screen on their first meaningful interaction. Recommendation: implement an async pre-fetch of student ID during sign-in flow, so timetable data is ready before the user navigates to it. Fallback to a skeleton state with estimated load time instead of blank screen.

Projected impact: recover ~7,700 users from first-session drop-off
2

Surface student services in onboarding flow — reduce to 1-tap from home

Student services was buried 3+ taps deep, invisible during onboarding. Given the 2.4× retention multiplier for students who activated it early, recommended promoting it to a first-session highlight card on the home screen and adding it as a persistent bottom-nav item. Supported by a contextual prompt during onboarding (Day 1 email or in-app nudge).

Projected impact: services activation from 19% → 38% within first session
3

Default campus map to user's enrolled campus zone with GPS confirmation

The map defaulted to a central zone requiring manual re-centering. Recommendation: use student profile data (school/department) to pre-select the most likely campus zone, with a one-tap confirmation prompt. Eliminates the first-use friction of having to orient the map manually before finding any room or building.

Projected impact: map session completion rate +17pp
✅

Two recommendations shipped as feature iterations

Recommendations 1 and 2 were adopted into the product roadmap and shipped as two distinct feature iterations, resulting in measurable 30-day retention improvements in the student services module — confirmed via post-release cohort analysis.

Chapter 07

Reflections

Doing rigorous product analytics inside a large institution — with real constraints, real stakeholders, and 35,000 real users — taught lessons that apply everywhere.

🔍

Data Granularity Changes Everything

Aggregate DAU charts told the team nothing. Cohort-level session analysis told them exactly where, when, and why students were leaving — and made the fix obvious.

🗣️

The Analytical Bridge Role

Being embedded between engineering and product leadership meant translating raw SQL output into language both sides could act on. Data without narrative doesn't move roadmaps.

🎯

Retention Levers Are Often Structural

The biggest retention problem wasn't the student services product — it was that students couldn't find it. Architecture problems often look like engagement problems in aggregate metrics.
